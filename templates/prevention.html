🛡️ Diabetes Prevention Guide

Evidence-based habits, lifestyle changes, and medical care to reduce your diabetes risk

⚠️ This guide is for informational purposes only. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider for personalised medical advice and treatment decisions.
🥗
Balanced Diet
Fill half your plate with vegetables, a quarter with whole grains, and a quarter with lean protein at every meal.
💧
Stay Hydrated
Drink 8–10 glasses of water daily. Water helps flush glucose and keeps metabolism efficient.
🚫
Avoid Sugary Drinks
Replace sodas, juices, and sweetened teas with water, black coffee, or green tea. Even 1 soda/day raises diabetes risk by 26%.
🍞
Limit Processed Foods
Packaged snacks, white bread, and fast food cause rapid glucose spikes. Switch to whole-food alternatives.
😴
Sleep 7–8 Hours
Poor sleep raises cortisol and disrupts insulin sensitivity. Aim for consistent sleep and wake times every day.
🍽️
Portion Control
Eat smaller, frequent meals rather than large ones. This prevents blood sugar spikes and improves insulin response.
Daily Habits Checklist
Ate at least 3 portions of vegetables today
Drank 8+ glasses of water
Avoided sugary drinks and desserts
Slept 7–8 hours last night
Ate breakfast within 1 hour of waking
🚶
Walk 30 Minutes Daily
A brisk 30-minute walk every day reduces diabetes risk by up to 30%. Even 10-minute walks after meals help glucose control.
💪
Strength Training
Muscle tissue absorbs glucose from blood. 2–3 sessions of resistance training per week significantly improves insulin sensitivity.
⚖️
Maintain Healthy Weight
Losing just 5–7% of body weight if overweight can cut diabetes risk by 58%. BMI under 25 is the target.
🪑
Reduce Sitting Time
Stand up every 30 minutes if you have a desk job. Prolonged sitting raises blood glucose even in active people.
🧘
Manage Stress
Chronic stress raises cortisol which directly increases blood glucose. Practice 10 minutes of meditation or deep breathing daily.
🚭
Quit Smoking
Smokers are 30–40% more likely to develop Type 2 diabetes. Quitting reduces risk to non-smoker levels within 5 years.

🩸
Blood Glucose
Fasting glucose should be below 100 mg/dL. 100–125 is prediabetes. Above 126 on two tests = diabetes. Check annually.
🫀
Blood Pressure
Target: below 120/80 mm Hg. High blood pressure and diabetes together multiply cardiovascular risk. Monitor monthly.
⚖️
BMI Tracking
Check BMI monthly. Target: 18.5–24.9. Waist circumference above 80cm (women) or 90cm (men) also signals risk.
🧪
HbA1c Test
HbA1c shows your average blood glucose over 3 months. Below 5.7% is normal. 5.7–6.4% is prediabetes. Get tested yearly.
🫁
Cholesterol
High LDL cholesterol with diabetes is especially dangerous. Check lipid profile every 6–12 months.

Annual Checkups
See your doctor at least once a year for a full metabolic panel including glucose, cholesterol, kidney and liver function.
💊
Take Medication as Prescribed
If prescribed Metformin or other diabetes medications, never skip doses. Consistent medication reduces HbA1c by 1–2%.
👁️
Eye Examination
Diabetes damages retinal blood vessels. Get an annual dilated eye exam to catch diabetic retinopathy early.
🦷
Dental Care
Gum disease worsens blood sugar control. Visit the dentist every 6 months and brush + floss twice daily.
👣
Foot Care
Diabetes affects circulation in feet. Check feet daily for cuts or sores. Wear comfortable shoes and never go barefoot outdoors.
🧬
Know Your Family History
If a parent or sibling has diabetes, your risk doubles. Tell your doctor so they can recommend earlier and more frequent screening.
