How the Bayesian Digital Twin Works
1

Data Preprocessing

Raw clinical data (Glucose, BMI, Blood Pressure, Insulin) from the Pima Indian Diabetes dataset is converted into categorical lifestyle nodes: Diet, Exercise, Sleep, Stress, Medication, BMI Level, and Insulin Sensitivity.

2

Causal Graph (DAG)

A Directed Acyclic Graph encodes causal relationships: Sleep affects Stress, Diet and Exercise affect Insulin Sensitivity, all of which causally influence GlucoseLevel. This is not correlation — it is causal reasoning.

3

Bayesian Parameter Learning

Conditional Probability Tables (CPTs) are learned from data using the BDeu Bayesian estimator with Laplace smoothing, which handles class imbalance and prevents the model from always predicting the majority class.

4

Variable Elimination Inference

Given your lifestyle evidence, Variable Elimination computes the exact posterior probability P(GlucoseLevel = High | evidence). This is probabilistic causal inference, not a black-box neural network.

5

What-If Simulation

By changing the evidence (e.g. switching Medication from No to Yes), you perform do-calculus intervention: P(GlucoseLevel | do(Medication=Yes)). This shows true causal effect, not just correlation.
